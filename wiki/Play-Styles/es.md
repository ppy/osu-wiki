Esta página explica las diferentes maneras de jugar Osu!

![](Osu.gif "fig:Osu.gif") osu! Standard
========================================

![](Playstyles M.png "fig:Playstyles M.png") Mouse-only (solo ratón)
--------------------------------------------------------------------

Esta fue una vez la única manera de jugar osu! standard hasta que los desarrolladores añadieron otros métodos. También conocido como pure-mouse, en este modo de juego los jugadores mueven el cursor con el ratón y también dan clic a las notas, siguen sliders y giran spinners solo con los clics de este. Altamente considerado como el método más difícil de jugar, los jugadores de este método que tienen un alto rango en los rankings son bien respetados por la comunidad.

El overlay de teclas muestra los clics izquierdo/derecho como M1 y M2.

![](Playstyles M.png "fig:Playstyles M.png")![](Playstyles M.png "fig:Playstyles M.png") Dual Mouse
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Duel Mouse es un estilo de juego en el que el jugador tiene un ratón en cada mano, usando uno para dar clic y el otro para apuntar/moverse. Dual mouse se consideraba como un modo "de broma" para la mayoría hasta Mayo del 2013, cuando un canadiense llamado ![](Ca.gif "fig:Ca.gif") [Azer](http://osu.ppy.sh/u/2155578) empezó a obtener posiciones en mapas como [With a Dance Number](http://osu.ppy.sh/s/29691) y [The Big Black](http://osu.ppy.sh/s/41823) usando Dual Mouse.

![](Playstyles M.png "fig:Playstyles M.png")![](Playstyles K.png "fig:Playstyles K.png") Mouse + Keyboard (Ratón + Teclado)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Este el el modo de juego más popular para osu! standar porque usa el hardware básico de una computadora, que son el ratón y un teclado. Los jugadores de este estilo juegan casi exactamente de la misma forma que los jugadores mouse-only con la diferencia de que usan dos teclas del teclado z y x por defecto) en lugar del ratón. Algunos jugadores usan solo el teclado para dar clic mientras otros solo cambian al teclado al hacer streams (cadena de notas muy rápida) y usan los clics del ratón para otras circunstancias.

El overlay de teclas muestra el presionar el teclado como K1 y K2.

![](Playstyles T.png "fig:Playstyles T.png") Tablet tap o Tablet-only (Solo tableta)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

<img src="Osu!tablet.jpg" title="fig:osu!tablet" alt="osu!tablet" width="150" /> Un modo de juego para aquellos que poseen una tableta gráfica (digitalizadora). Estos jugadores usan un lápiz de tableta en lugar de un ratón para mover el cursor pero dan clic con el mismo lápiz dentro de la superficie de la tableta misma sin necesidad de un teclado.

El overlay de entrada mostrará los golpes como M1.

![](Playstyles T.png "fig:Playstyles T.png")![](Playstyles K.png "fig:Playstyles K.png") Tablet + Keyboard (Tableta + Teclado)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Un estilo de juego popular para quienes tienen una tableta gráfica (digitalizadora). Estos jugadores usan el lápiz de la tableta para mover el cursor y dan clic usando el teclado o los botones de la tableta. Algunas veces referido como "tapx", la comunidad ha usado este término para describir cómo los streams únicamente con tableta funcionan. Para hacer streams como un jugador que solo usa tableta, alternas entre presionar con el lápiz en la tableta y presionando una tecla asignada (porque solo el botón secundario funcionará, y 'x' es el botón secundario por defecto).

![](Playstyles TC.png "fig:Playstyles TC.png") Touchscreen (Pantalla Táctil)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Uno de los posibles modos de juego para los jugadores que poseen un monitor táctil, ya sea de escritorio, portátil o tableta. Los jugadores tocan la pantalla para mover el cursor y dar clic. Este modo de juego puede parecer ligeramente similar a jugar osu!stream/osu!droid pues tienes que tocar la pantalla directamente en lugar que el hardware. Algunos jugadores oprtan por este modo de juego cuando no tienen una tableta gráfica.

El overlay de entrada mostrará tus toques solo como M1.

![](Playstyles TC.png "fig:Playstyles TC.png")![](Playstyles K.png "fig:Playstyles K.png") Touchscreen + Keyboard
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A play style used by people that own a touch-enabled monitor/laptop screen, this play style is mostly adopted by touchscreen players who cannot perform [streams](Streams "wikilink") using only the touchscreen. This play style can be similar to Tablet + Keyboard as some monitors allow for a pen to be used instead of your finger.

Other styles
------------

**Laptop touchpad/trackpad**: While being theoretically possible, most players prefer not to use a trackpad because of the extreme difficulty involved (specifically, controlling the cursor movement on the limited space of the laptop touchpad/trackpad). The trackpad is used (instead of a mouse) to navigate the in-game cursor.

**Joystick**: A creative way to control osu!cursor. Player hold and move the joystick around to move the osu!cursor and clicks on the trigger/face buttons(depending on player's joystick design) or keyboard keys to register the beats. It is least preferred because the skill required to control the joystick movement and most don't own it unless they also play other games ([World of Warplanes](wikipedia:World_of_Warplanes "wikilink") for example) .

**Metal plate**: A special way to replace the keyboard for clicking beats. This method is introduced by [geckogates](https://osu.ppy.sh/u/geckogates) and requires some wiring and electronic knowledge to build. You will attach a wire to your clicking fingers (connected to keyboard's circuit for z,x) and will touch the metal plates to register beats.

![](Taiko.gif "fig:Taiko.gif") Taiko
====================================

![](Playstyles K.png "fig:Playstyles K.png") Keyboard
-----------------------------------------------------

This was once the only way to play Taiko until developers added other methods. To put it simply, you press your assigned keyboard keys to "hit" the drum parts; x,c for drum center (Red Don) and z,v for drum rim (Blue Katsu). Usually, skilled Taiko players use their Taiko controllers (either bought or self-made) to play, most players use this play style should they not have the luxury of owning a Taiko drum.

There are two main styles of setting your keys placement in the keyboard, <span style="color:blue">K</span><span style="color:red">DD</span><span style="color:blue">K</span> \[Taiko default\] or (<span style="color:red">DD</span><span style="color:blue">KK</span>/<span style="color:blue">KK</span><span style="color:red">DD</span>). These represent the combination of how you set your keys to hit the notes. There may be other, weird combinations, but they are almost never used.

For key-binding, the default is zxcv (<span style="color:blue">K</span><span style="color:red">DD</span><span style="color:blue">K</span>).

Taiko controller
----------------

The default controller for the [Taiko no Tatsujin](wikipedia:Taiko_no_Tatsujin "wikilink") series([Taiko Drum Master](wikipedia:Taiko_Drum_Master "wikilink") for US). Most skilled players play using the Taiko controller bought or created from scratch. Player will carry each hand respectively a taiko/drum stick and hit the surface or rim of the drum to register the note based on where it is hit. Most Taiko controllers connect to the computer by using Bluetooth connector should there be no USB connecter.

Other styles
------------

**Gamepad**: One of the possible play-style in console variation of Taiko no Tatsujin series(including Taiko Drum Master). Players register Don/Katsu by pressing the face \[R Don\], arrow \[L Don\] and upper buttons \[L/R Katsu\].

![](Ctb.gif "fig:Ctb.gif") Catch the Beat
=========================================

![](Playstyles K.png "fig:Playstyles K.png") Keyboard
-----------------------------------------------------

The most commonly used and default play-style for this mode. Players press left/right arrow to move the catcher, and also can hold down Left Shift to trigger Dash!(x2 speed) mode. As always, it is possible to edit the keys used; some players enjoy a/d for movement and spacebar/Right Shift for Dash! effect.

Joystick
--------

A possible play-style for players used to pixel-old arcade cabinets' control scheme. Players move the head of the joystick and press the face button/trigger/keyboard key to trigger the Dash! effect.

Gamepad
-------

A possible play-style for players which are comfortable playing with a gamepad. Players press the left/right arrow of the gamepad to move the catcher left/right and press then hold the face button to trigger the Dash! effect.

![](Mania.gif "fig:Mania.gif") osu!mania
========================================

![](Playstyles K.png "fig:Playstyles K.png") Keyboard
-----------------------------------------------------

[External guide for Mechanical Keyboard](http://www.daskeyboard.com/blog/mechanical-keyboard-guide/)
This was once the only way to play osu!mania until developers added other methods. Players place their fingers on the keyboard keys with respect to the key-bindings and currently playing Keys setting. The advantage of this play-style is that it can accommodate basically all the 4/5/6/7/8Keys setting. The only catch-point is getting used to it and regular keyboard's ghosting effect/membrane keys (unless you are using a decent gaming keyboard).

[DJMAX](wikipedia:DJMAX "wikilink")/[O2Jam](wikipedia:O2Jam "wikilink")/[Beatmania IIDX](wikipedia:Beatmania_IIDX "wikilink")/etc. (7K) arcade controller
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The play-style tuned for seasoned veterans at DJ-styled rhythm games. Most hardcore players of this discipline usually have their own personal arcade controller of their choice. Since 8Keys requires mapper's decision or Game Modifiers, most players owning these arcade controllers can handle up to 7Keys (max default) and 1 special.

Other styles
------------

**Dancepad**(4/8K): The default controller for [Dance Dance Revolution](wikipedia:Dance_Dance_Revolution "wikilink") series ([Stepmania](wikipedia:StepMania "wikilink") for community-driven). Players dance on the dancepad to input commands. Depending on dancepad and player's preference, it is possible to use the face buttons as input commands rather than the usual left/down/up/right arrow buttons. The biggest drawback is the maximum input at a time is four (hands and feet), which is very disadvantaging considering current beatmaps can go up to much more than that.

**Guitar/Drum set**(5/6K): The default controller for guitar-themed([Guitar Hero](wikipedia:Guitar_Hero "wikilink"))/drum-themed([Drummania](wikipedia:Drum_Mania "wikilink")) rhythm game. Players streams on their guitar/hit the drum or cymbals to input commands. Drum players have a disadvantage compared to guitar players because they can only input two commands(two hands) at a time without a special kick pedal.

**Para Para controller**(5K): The default controller for [Para Para Paradise](wikipedia:Para_Para_Paradise "wikilink") series. Players move their arms, elbows and hands straight above the proximity sensors to input commands based on hand position through the proximity sensors. The drawback is that you only have two arms to input commands, excluding the use of legs.
