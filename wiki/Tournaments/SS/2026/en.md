# Spanish Showdown 2026

![Spanish Showdown 2026 banner](img/banner.png)

Spanish Showdown 2026 (SS26) is the most well known spanish-only 1v1 open rank tournament, featuring low SR pools at lower stages for new players and a very high skill ceiling in later stages. Only the best will prevail.

## Tournament schedule

| Event | Timestamp |
| --: | :-- |
| Registration phase | 2026-02-02/2026-02-15 |
| Qualifiers showcase | 2026-02-15 |
| Qualifiers stage | 2026-02-16/2026-03-01 |
| Seed reveal & Group draw | 2026-03-01 |
| Group Stage | 2026-03-01/2026-03-15 |
| Round of 32 | 2026-03-16/2026-03-22 |
| Round of 16 | 2026-03-23/2026-03-29 |

| Finals | 2026-04-13/2026-04-19 |
| Grand Finals | 2026-04-19/2026-04-26 |

## Groups

Groups were made with a draw. Players were separated in 2 different groups using qualifier results as follows:

- First draft: Seeds 17-20, 25-28, 33-36, 41-44
- Second draft: Seeds 21-24, 29-32, 37-40, 45-48

Now that we had 4 "boxes" per draft, we rolled a D4 and went through the cards in order. If for example we rolled a 3, the third card would get chosen from the first box. If we have 3 cards and we rolled a four, we would go back to the first card, rolling the cards as we went. 4 groups came from each draft, formed by one player from each box. The metric used for ranking players inside a group are Points in favor (PIF) and Points against (PA). The difference of PIF - PA is the score of a player. In the case of a tie, qualifiers seed will be used to decide who's on top.

After the group stage, the two top players from each group go to round of 32 playoffs, assigning the seeds by doing an average seed for each group. Here's how it is done for each one of them:

## Ruleset

### General ruleset

1. Score v2 will be used.
2. Spanish-only tournament; only players with the Spanish flag on their profile may participate.
3. Open Rank.
4. Scores count even if you fail the map (NF enforced).
5. Freemod is only available on Tiebreakers.
6. The winner of the roll will choose the order of picks or bans; the opponent will choose the order of the remaining option.
7. From Group Stage to Ro16, players will have one ban to be applied at the beginning of the match.
8. From QF onwards, there will be two bans for each player; the player who bans first after the roll will ban second in the ban round that occurs after the 4th point (AB - BA).
9. Group Stage, Ro32: Best of 9.
10. Ro16, Quarterfinals: Best of 11.
11. Semifinals, Finals: Best of 13.
12. Grand Finals: Best of 15.

### General structure

1. This is a Spanish national 1v1 tournament for the osu!standard mode. Players may sign up regardless of their rank.
2. All players must be in the tournament's Discord server for its duration; otherwise, they will be disqualified.
3. Score V2 will always be used as the scoring metric.
4. Players will be seeded based on the results of the qualifiers phase.
5. The qualifiers phase will consist of playing a 10-map mappool once in a lobby.
6. In grand finals, a bracket reset is possible if the player coming from the losers' bracket wins the first match.
7. All schedules will be provided in Spanish Peninsular time, as it is the official time zone for most of the Spanish territory.
8. Rescheduling to days inconvenient for the staff and then failing to show up at the agreed time will lead to immediate disqualification.
9. Tournament staff members cannot play in the tournament, with the exception of commentators, streamers, and GFX creators. Only eliminated players may participate in refereeing duties.

### Mappool structure

1. Qualifiers (6.9*): 4 NoMod, 2 Hidden, 2 HardRock, 2 DoubleTime.
2. Group Stage (6.3*), Ro32 (6.5*), Ro16 (6.8*): 5 NoMod, 2 Hidden, 2 HardRock, 3 DoubleTime, 1 Tiebreaker.
3. QF (7.1*), SF (7.4*): 6 NoMod, 3 Hidden, 3 HardRock, 4 DoubleTime, 1 Tiebreaker.
4. F (7.7*), GF (8.0*): 6 NoMod, 4 Hidden, 4 HardRock, 4 DoubleTime, 1 Tiebreaker.

### Qualifiers stage

1. During qualifiers, after signing up for a lobby, 15 minutes before it starts, you will be notified through discord and 5 minutes before it you will receive in-game invitations (please ensure you are online).
2. In case of not receiving the invitation or a sudden disconnection, contact the referee assigned to the room.
3. Default lobby schedules will be available on the tournament website. If none of the lobbies suit you, you may request a new lobby in the dedicated Discord channel (#qualifiers-schedule).
4. In the room, you will play the maps in order (NoMod -> Hidden -> HardRock -> DoubleTime) (10 maps).
5. The mappool can only be played once.
6. The scoring system used will be Z-Score. Refer to [this wiki page](/wiki/Tournaments/Common_seeding_methods) in order to check out how it works.
7. Qualifiers results will remain private throughout the process; lobby links will not be public either.
8. If a player is found sniping mp links from qualifier rooms, they will be irrevocably disqualified without the possibility of appeal if they are participating in the tournament.
9. If someone intentionally decides not to play a map (remaining AFK for the entire duration), they will be penalized with a x0.75 multiplier to all their qualifier scores. This penalty will be reviewed on a case-by-case basis.

### Playoffs stage

1. The room will be created, and invitations will be sent to both players. If you do not receive an invitation, let the staff know.
2. Players are required to be punctual. Therefore, if a player is 10 minutes late from the established time, they will lose their bans. If they are 15 minutes late, the victory will be awarded to the opponent.
3. You have until 12 hours before both the new and old match times to change the schedule (flexibility for this rule will be reviewed case-by-case; staff reserves the right to approve them).
4. In case of reschedule conflicts, if neither player has changed the original match time and neither shows up, both players will be disqualified (we reserve the right to study each case).
5. Each player may request a warmup of less than or equal to 4 minutes.
6. When both players are in the room, both will use the !roll command. The player with the highest number may choose the order of picks or bans.
7. From Group Stage to Ro16, players will have one ban to be applied at the beginning of the match.
8. From QF onwards, there will be two bans; the player who bans first after the roll will ban second in the ban round that occurs after the 4th point (AB - BA).
9. Players have 1 minute to pick a map. If a map is not chosen within that minute, and it is not due to connection issues, the opponent will choose the map. However, this will not change the pick order.
10. Once a player has chosen a map, players will have 1 minute to be ready. The map will start when both players are ready.
11. Players may use mods in the tiebreaker.
12. In case of a disconnection, there will be several procedures: if there is proof of the score, it will count. If there is no proof or it is too ambiguous, the opponent will receive the point. If the disconnection occurs shortly after the map starts, the point will be aborted and the map will be played again.
