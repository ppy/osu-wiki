The osu!taiko World Cup is back with its 2026 iteration, this year also featuring a Group Stage. Read on to catch up with the latest standings before this weekend's Round of 16 matches!

![](/wiki/Tournaments/TWC/2026/img/twc2026-banner.jpg)

The group stage returns to the osu!taiko World Cup, featuring a round-robin format with each team playing against the other two teams in their group. This guarantees a minimum of 3 matches for every Challenger team, creating a more competitive experience and a more exciting start to the tournament. Even if a team places last in their group, they will still advance to the bracket stage, starting in the losers' bracket in next weekend's Round of 16.

To explain the numbers in the following tables: **PF** (Points For) reflects a team's total scoring output, while **PA** (Points Against) captures what they've given up defensively. The resulting **PD** (Point Difference) — the gap between the two — offers a quick snapshot of overall performance and often serves as the deciding factor when teams are tied for wins.

## Match highlights

### ::{ flag=AU }:: Australia vs. ::{ flag=NL }:: The Netherlands

The Group Stage mappool was on the easy side for what was already the easiest round of a stacked tournament, so we saw some very high-level matches filled with **near-SS battles and 4-way full combos**, but even in a round with more tiebreakers than 5–0 sweeps, one match stood out: the battle for Group H between Australia (#11) and the Netherlands (#23). Both extremely strong accuracy teams, they made a pool of relatively friendly Inner Onis look like Futsuus for a full hour, taking each other to the limit repeatedly until only one team remained victorious.

It started fairly uneventful, with the favourites Australia winning their first pick of [Hidra-Xjeil - MilK(Holy Grail Remix)](https://osu.ppy.sh/beatmapsets/2523185#taiko/5571806) (FM2) along with the Netherlands' [EGOIST - Lovely Icecream Princess Sweetie](https://osu.ppy.sh/beatmapsets/2523329#taiko/5572177) (DT1) selection to go up 2-0, before this match hit another gear of intensity that it would never leave. Australia picked [Mameyudoufu - Point](https://osu.ppy.sh/beatmapsets/2523184#taiko/5571805) (HR1), and entered the tricky final section with 4 100s to an opposing 7. What happened next... you should just see for yourself.

With this miracle point for the Netherlands, so close many spectators thought it was supposed to be a tie, they pounced on the slightest hint of weakness, picking [HyuN feat. YURI - Disorder](https://osu.ppy.sh/beatmapsets/2523470#taiko/5572580) (HR2), one of the most consistent SS maps in the pool. Naturally, both teams tied with a combined one 100 each! World Cup rules mean the map has to be replayed until one team finishes with more score than the other, and on the runback, an impressive display of nerve control gave ::{ flag=NL }:: [Gibiji](https://osu.ppy.sh/users/21058672) and ::{ flag=NL }:: [Cookie_Tree](https://osu.ppy.sh/users/502722) the maximum possible team score of 2,120,000, beating a second straight 1x100 run from Australia and tying up the match after 4 picks!

Australia's next pick of [Shion Kanzaki - Ganglion](https://osu.ppy.sh/beatmapsets/2523227#taiko/5571893) (HD2) saw the Netherlands ahead for the most of the map, coming into the final tech section ahead by exactly 3 points, but both players hitting a single 100 on the same note saved Australia's pick, before ::{ flag=NL }:: [Cookie_Tree](https://osu.ppy.sh/users/502722)'s 2x100 HDHR FC on [WangleLine - Super Kitten Travel Hex](https://osu.ppy.sh/beatmapsets/2523236#taiko/5571916) (FM1) proceeded to level it back up at 3–3. The founding father of the 1-key mania scene, ::{ flag=AU }:: [Jaye](https://osu.ppy.sh/users/4841352), proceeded to pick into his own skillset, HD1, with an original track from [1KWC (1-Key World Cup)](/wiki/Tournaments/JOKE/1KWC_2025), [MYUKKE. - Seal the Deal](https://osu.ppy.sh/beatmapsets/2523472#taiko/5572583), that he'd brought into the world... and missed! With everyone else FCing as usual, the Netherlands had one match point.

[femtanyl - IT'S TIME](https://osu.ppy.sh/beatmapsets/2523224#taiko/5571889) (NM5) was the final pick, and the Netherlands hit zero 100s to Australia's one... but a single mid-stream miss brought us to a tiebreaker. This week's TB, [Kagetora. vs. ikaruga_nex - Gott der Apokalypse](https://osu.ppy.sh/beatmapsets/2523240#taiko/5571925), is on the simple side for an osu!taiko tiebreaker, but that just means it becomes a sort of FM3 pick: who can stack the most mods on without destroying themselves via nerves? The answer, resoundingly for once, was Australia, putting on the team score of the weekend with **a double HDHR full combo**.

A special mention has to go to rookie-of-the-year frontrunner ::{ flag=AU }:: [Frostetic](https://osu.ppy.sh/users/30388979), with a perfect record of 10 out of 10 full combos despite all the pressure! With this, and a later win over Colombia, Australia secured the Group H crown, and a spot in the upper bracket in the Round of 16, where they face the one thing scarier than Group Stage Netherlands: 13-time champion **Japan**.
