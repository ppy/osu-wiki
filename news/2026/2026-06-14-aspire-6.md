From the haze of memory, a monolith returns. Aspire to the greatest heights that your creativity can offer with a cherished companion in tow, and set forth to remake the beatmapping world in your collective image. Break everything, and build it beautiful again.

![](https://assets.ppy.sh/contests/286/header.jpg)

Welcome back to Aspire, the beatmapping contest that focuses on just one thing: *creativity*. For those who may be unfamiliar, [Aspire](/wiki/Contests/Aspire) is a contest where the [ranking criteria](/wiki/Ranking_criteria) is thrown completely out of the window — the sky's the limit with what mappers can do with their craft.

The last iteration of Aspire saw the creation of some of the most creative maps in the history of the game. The previous winners include [Time Traveler](https://osu.ppy.sh/beatmapsets/1236988#osu/2573493), which honoured the song's title by throwing the player backwards and forwards through time, as well as [XNOR XNOR XNOR](https://osu.ppy.sh/beatmapsets/1236927#osu/2571051), which featured a challenge not just as a beatmap, but also as an ARG puzzle. Another favourite was [Space Invaders](https://osu.ppy.sh/beatmapsets/1237212#fruits/2571609), a map which basically is its own game mode.

If you want to have a look at some more Aspire maps [here is the news post covering the top entries of Aspire V](http://news/2020-07-21-aspire-v-finals-stage-voting).

## Format

Once again, we have some changes to the format of this year's Aspire:

First off, **entries will feature teams of up to 2 people**. The amount of effort and creativity required to compete in Aspire contests is rapidly approaching levels that would be a tall order for just a single competitor.

On top of that, we understand that there are concerns of Aspire becoming a "storyboarding competition". Since Aspire is about using every available resource available to you, it would be a shame to completely discourage the use of storyboards, but we also understand that storyboarding isn't accessible to everyone. We hope that by allowing teams of 2 people, the workload of mapping and storyboarding (or even just mapping!) can be delegated, opening the doors for even greater spectacles.

If you're in need of a partner for Aspire you can look for a buddy over on the [Mappers' Guild](https://mappersguild.com/aspire) website.

Of course, if you're the sort of insane person able to produce such magnificent work alone, you're also free to enter solo if you wish. [The registration form can be found here](https://form-auth.ppy.sh/form/2026-aspire-6-registrations).

Second, **we will be streamlining how we approach voting and categories**. In the previous iteration, we introduced categories as an attempt to bring out new avenues of competing in Aspire beyond just "break the game". However we didn't quite hit the mark last time.

While categories were successful in encouraging mappers to try new angles of creativity, one main criticism was how some categories had to be padded out to even exist.

This time, we will be heavily streamlining the categories into two broad categories. More details on the categories are written below!

The voting and judging stage in the previous iteration was also rather convoluted and excessive. As Aspire is primarily a contest about wowing the community, there will be no internal judging beyond a basic screening process — the winners will be decided on community voting only. Similar to the seasonal art contests, we will also allow you to vote for more than one map per category.

There is deliberately some flexibility with categories and prizes, as we want to provide the most sensible voting process depending on the number of entries we receive for each game mode.

We hope that these changes will help to improve upon the pain points from the last contest.

## Categories

There are two main categories for Aspire 6:

### Visual Spectacle

Slider art, storyboards, and just raw aesthetics — this category is for the maps that aren't necessarily playable, but are for sure a sight to behold. This category fits best for the traditional Aspire entries like [Solace of Oblivion](https://osu.ppy.sh/beatmapsets/594751).

### Innovative Gameplay

We mustn't forget that we're playing a game at the end of the day — this category is for the maps that are just as much as experience to play as they are cutting edge. Maps such as [Space Invaders](https://osu.ppy.sh/beatmapsets/1237212#fruits/2571609) fit best for this category.

Depending on the number of entries we receive, we may combine game modes and their categories together. We may also consider awarding a mapper's pick for each category depending on the votes received. We will review this internally after submissions have closed.

## Rules

The rules for Aspire 6 are as follows:

### Beatmap rules

- This contest is open to **all** game modes.
- The ranking criteria **does not** apply to this contest.
- You may only map songs from the [Featured Artists](https://osu.ppy.sh/beatmaps/artists) listing with the **following restrictions**:
- You may **not** choose the following songs:
  - Camellia - Arche
  - Camellia - Treaty of Peace between GlitchHop and Moombahcore
  - Frums - XNOR XNOR XNOR
  - Function Phantom - Algebra
  - Helblinde - The Solace of Oblivion
  - IAHN - Transform
  - KNOWER - Time Traveler
  - LeaF - Evanescent
  - LeaF - Mopemope
  - OISHII - ONIGIRI FREEWAY
  - RiraN - Unshakable
  - Teminite & MDK - Space Invaders
- You may **not** make any edits to the `.mp3` files provided on the Featured Artists listing.
- You may **not** choose any songs that have been added to the Featured Artists listing after **14 June 2026**.
- Your entry does not need to be passable, however, your entry may be ineligible for certain categories or prizes if it is not possible to be passed by a human player.
- Your entry must consist of **exactly one difficulty** that contains an interactive experience — storyboard-only entries aren't allowed.

### Operational rules

- You may enter as a team of up to 2 people — [please register your team via the form here](https://form-auth.ppy.sh/form/2026-aspire-6-registrations).
  - You may also enter as a solo contestant if you wish.
- Any submitted entry that doesn't have a registration tied to it will be automatically disqualified.
- Entries must be submitted by the person who registered the team in the form.
- Teams and submissions must remain anonymous for the duration of the contest. Please ensure all beatmap elements, including storyboard elements, are anonymised.
- Your entry **must not** be uploaded to the osu! website via the Beatmap Submission System (BSS).
- Your entry must be submitted in `.osz` format in the contest listing.

Any entries that break these rules may be disqualified from the contest.

### Disclaimer regarding glitches and bugs

Aspire has historically been a time to showcase groundbreaking features and gameplay — including glitches.

While glitches and bugs can be very entertaining to witness, anything that is *too broken* may not be supported in the future. We're primarily here to have fun, but we don't want that to come at the cost of leaving parts of the game in a permanently broken state.

Any exploits used in osu!(stable) may not be supported in osu!(lazer). Any exploits used in either client may be fixed in the future depending on the severity. This is the case even if a map showcasing such exploits wins or gets moved to Loved.

You are free to map primarily for either osu!(lazer) or osu!(stable). You may let us know which client you prefer your beatmap to be showcased in when registering your entry.

## Voting

The winners of Aspire 6 will be decided from community voting only.

After entries close, the qualifying entries will be anonymised and uploaded to the [osu!team account](https://osu.ppy.sh/users/4341397). A playlist will also be provided for the submissions which aren't *too unplayable*.

Each game mode will have up to two contest listings (one for each category) where you will be able to submit your votes.

The contest organisers may choose to reduce the number of categories for a game mode if there aren't enough entries for that game mode, either by having a general category for that game mode or cancelling prizes for that game mode altogether.

You will be able to vote for more than one entry for each category — the exact number of votes available will be dependent on the number of entries. You are **not** required to play the maps in order to vote for them. The most voted maps of each category will be eligible for rewards.

We will also be tallying up the votes from mappers — if you have a ranked mapset or guest difficulty at the time voting ends, you will be counted as a mapper. In the event that the votes from mappers and non-mappers are notably different, we'll likely award a "mapper's pick" prize alongside the main one.

## Prizes

The prizes for this contest are as follows:

**Aspirant**: Awarded to the winners of each Aspire category.

Both participants will receive:

- Elite Mapper: Aspirant title (or special increment for existing Elite Mappers)
- Aspire 6 winner badge
- 1 year of osu!supporter

**Runner Up**: Awarded to runners up and honourable mentions of each category.

Both participants will receive:

- Aspire 6 runner-up badge
- 6 months of osu!supporter
- 2 contest points

All prize-winning entries will also be added to Loved, as long as their maps are passable by a human player.

The ownership of all entries will be transferred to one of the entrants after the contest ends — we will contact you to ask who will be the main set owner after the contest ends.
