Project Loved is here to save you from the April showers!

![](/wiki/shared/news/banners/project-loved-2.jpg)

Welcome to this month's round of Project Loved picks! Are we new to the game, just checking the news for the first time, or just unsure in general? Project Loved hosts community voting polls each month to decide which beatmaps get added to the [Loved category](https://osu.ppy.sh/beatmapsets?s=loved). All of the maps are chosen by the [Loved captains](https://loved.sh/team) of their respective game modes. Each beatmap banner in this news post will redirect you to the respective poll. Alternatively you can view them all directly on the [Project Loved forum](https://osu.ppy.sh/community/forums/120). The voting process lasts for 10 days, so don't forget to vote! More info can be found on the [wiki page](/wiki/Community/Project_Loved) and [website](https://loved.sh/).

And yes, you too can help us out! Simply hop on over to the Loved website and submit any maps you feel are deserving of our attention. You can even review suggestions from other players!

A jumpstream (/d͡ʒʌmpstɹiːm/, plural: jumpstreams) consists of a string of single notes punctuated by double notes, also referred to as "jumps". Why "jump", do you ask? To figure that out, we have to go back in history, back when we were hitting arrows with our feet... namely, popularized by the [Dance Dance Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dance_Dance_Revolution) series. An overwhelmingly common way to hit double notes with your feet is to jump on those notes with your feet, therefore performing a "jump". That's why double notes are called "jumps". Yeah, that's it.

An exercise for the astute reader: what is a "hand"? Well, through the same foot logic, how do you hit triple notes? You jump on 2 notes with your 2 feet, and hit a third one with a hand! Hence, the "hand" naming for triple notes. So you may ask: what are quadruple notes called then? They're just called quads. That's it. No one refers to them differently. Yeah. I've only really heard the term "pizza" to refer to a septuple note in 7K, but as far as 4K is concerned, you'll only have to deal with quads at most.

You now know what jumps, hands, and quads are. What are streams then? They are strings of single notes. What do you get when you string streams with jumps? Jumpstreams! Woo! Yippee!

Introducing [Crash-Landing Rendezvous](https://osu.ppy.sh/beatmapsets/2307516#mania/4935112), mapped by [Irone OSU](https://osu.ppy.sh/users/10678230)!

[Irone OSU](https://osu.ppy.sh/users/10678230)'s mapping style is one to watch in the Ranked scene; he's a mapper who simplifies the music to fit a concept, delivering an experience that's both comfortable and guaranteed to be fun for players. The map we're introducing today was featured as an HB3 in the [Round of 32](https://osu.ppy.sh/beatmaps/packs/P271) of the [osu!mania 7K World Cup 2025](/wiki/Tournaments/MWC/2025_7K). We believe it maximises the fun derived from the diverse patterns of the "full flavour" genre. Also, note that it was released as an osu! original in the [osu!taiko Mapping World Cup 2024](https://osu.ppy.sh/community/forums/topics/1865674?n=1).

If you'd like to support Irone OSU's first step into the Loved scene and see more maps like this, please vote "Yes"!
